my master thesis

Introduction to Game Audio

graded with 1,0

The topic of my master thesis was "The development of a prototype in Unity as a guide for the implementation of audio in games".

This resulted in a written guide in which I dealt with the topics of HRTF, occlusion, reverb, ambisonics, distance effects and dynamic music, among others. In addition, I developed a Unity project that I used to demonstrate the practical implementation of these topics.

Software

Nowadays there is a wide range of tools that allow developers to realize their dream game, each of which has its advantages and disadvantages. They are used for the development of a variety of games, making the video game market very diverse.


For the creation of the prototype I decided to use the game engine Unity, as it is widely used and suitable for beginners.

In addition, I used the audio middlewares FMOD and Wwise and the Spatial Audio plugin Resonance Audio.

Topics

Prototype

The various game audio themes are assigned to rooms in the prototype, through which the player can walk with the character in first-person perspective.

A menu in the Unity editor allows the user to select which sound engine (Unity's built-in engine, FMOD or Wwise) should be used to render the audio.
